# Transcript Writing Guidance

Rules for generating roommate argument transcripts.

## Format
- 60–75 words, ~30 seconds when read aloud
- Lines are very short and snappy (1–2 sentences max), rapid fire
- This is the active dispute — they are fighting RIGHT NOW, not recapping
- NEVER address the other person by name, letter ("A", "B"), or any pronoun that implies you can see them

## Tone: structure is HOOK → FIGHT → PUNCHLINE
- LINE 1: punchy funny hook that states the problem (see below)
- LINES 2–10: REAL ANGRY CONFLICT. People interrupting, defending themselves, escalating. Frustrated. Petty. Specific. This is NOT a comedy sketch — it's a real fight that happens to be funny because of who these two people are
- LAST 1–2 LINES: the punchline. One killer absurd line that ends it

The contrast between the two personalities IS the comedy. You do not need to write jokes. A neat-freak saying "I bond with a mop every day!" is still a joke — don't do that. They should say "I've cleaned that bathroom FOUR TIMES this week." That's real. That's what makes it land.

Bad middle lines (too jokey, kills tension):
- "Bonding over issues is how we grow!"
- "I bond with a mop every day!"
- "It adds character!"

Good middle lines (real conflict, personality shows through):
- "The lease says NO PETS. I have that in writing."
- "It's been two months. That's not a phase, that's a lifestyle."
- "I'm not asking you to get rid of it, I'm asking you to walk it."

## Audio-only: no visual references
The audience can ONLY hear this. They cannot see anything — no characters, no room, no props.
- NEVER reference something the listener can't know from audio alone (e.g. "it's just a temporary roommate!" when talking about a dog that's visibly in the room — the listener doesn't know there's a dog unless someone says so)
- Every joke, reference, and insult must land from audio alone

## Opening line: direct accusation, not a question or metaphor
The first line must be a clear, direct accusation that states exactly what the other person did wrong. It should land like a punch — specific, angry, unmistakable.
- Bad: "Did a cheese factory explode in your room, or is this just cat chaos?" (a question — unclear, too clever, listener doesn't know what's happening)
- Bad: "Your dog trashed the bathroom and it's never outside." (too dry, no energy)
- Bad: "Another mess in the bathroom again!" (what mess? caused by what?)
- Good: "Your cat has turned this entire apartment into a BIOHAZARD and you don't even care!"
- Good: "Your dog pooped in the hallway AGAIN and I'm the one cleaning it up — AGAIN!"
- Good: "I paid half this rent and I can't use the bathroom because of your cats!"

The opener must be: ACCUSATION + SPECIFIC PROBLEM + anger/energy. Not a joke. Not a question. A direct statement of what they did.
